Jaylen Waddle's Career

This page is about the career of Jaylen Waddle, an NFL wide receiver.


Early Life and High School

Jaylen Waddle grew up in Houston and attended Episcopal High School in Bellaire, Texas. He grew up playing wide receiver, returned kicks, and ran track. He became known for quick acceleration, great jukes, and his play making. His highlights were able to draw offers from great programs. Waddle came to the decision to commit to Alabama due to its coaching, wide receiver development, and its competition. This was a great decision and the start of his rise in Alabama.

College Career

After deciding to play for the University of Alabama, Waddle was able to make a quick impression and impact on the team. Both as a receiver and a return specialist on the team, making impressive catches on offense and special teams. Although he was in a great receiver room, he was able to earn snaps due to his reliable hands and elite yardage after catch. He suffered an ankle injury his senior year but was able to bounce back on the field for the national championship game. He declared for the NFL Draft, where he was viewed by many teams as a playmaker with elite speed.

NFL Career

aylen Waddle was drafted sixth overall by the Miami Dolphins, Waddle was able to make a quick transition from college to the NFL. With the Miami Dolphins, he was reunited with his former quarterback Tua. This was able to help both the players due to their history playing together, Waddle had 106 receptions his rookie season becoming a reliable receiver and a dangerous deep threat. As Miami has added more speed to their offense, Waddle has been making downfield plays. He has been a red-zone threat and a key threat in the backfield showing growth each season.
